Presentation format Public
  • The length of a presentation will be maximum 60 minutes. Preferably 50 minutes with 10 minutes to Q & A.
  • The length of a lightning talk will be maximum 10 minutes including Q & A.
Title Public
  • Select an expressive title that captures the contents of your talk well without being too long.
  • Remember that the title must be attractive and should make a reader curious.
Abstract Public
  • Give a concise description of the contents and goals of your talk.
  • The abstract will be published in the conference programme.
  • It should not be longer than 300 words.
Language Public
  • The talk can be given in Norwegian or English
  • The slides may be in English even if you present the talk in Norwegian.
  • We generally recommends that you hold the talk in the language you are most comfortable with. In our experience the talk will be better then.

    Level Public
    More information about how we classify the talks can be found here.

    Skill levels for talks

    Introductory

    A basic introduction to a subject. Assume no knowledge of the subject.

    Intermediate

    Expecting more than basic knowledge of a subject, but not yet advanced.

    Advanced

    Good knowledge of the subject.

    Hardcore

    Assume a fair amount of knowledge in the audience. Do not spend more than 5-7 minutes on setting the stage, and avoid introductory material. Should ideally focus on a single subject, for instance "classloaders", "debugging", "bytecode generation", "the monoid"... The deeper and more hardcore, the better.

    Outline
    This information will be used by the program committee for reviewing the detailed contents. The outline should be a table of contents of the talk, with a few keywords for each section, and with a rough estimate of the time spent on each.
    Highlight summary Public

    Description of the contents of your talk in only a few sentences. The highlight summary can be used in teaser presentations on the web prior to the conference.
